The Musical Legend

Philly Bongoley Lutaaya (19 October 1951 â€“ 15 December 1989) was a Ugandan musician who was the first prominent Ugandan to give a human face to HIV/AIDS. He became a national hero because he was the first Ugandan to declare that he was HIV-positive. Before dying of AIDS, Lutaaya spent his remaining time writing songs about his battle with AIDS, releasing his last album "Alone and Frightened," including his famous song "Alone." He toured churches and schools throughout Uganda to spread a message of prevention and hope. Lutaaya's music incorporates western pop music style and remains popular in Uganda.

The Fight Against HIV Prejudice in Uganda

Philly Lutaaya, a Ugandan music star, was diagnosed with HIV in 1989. He became the first high-profile Ugandan to go public with his diagnosis, challenging the fear and prejudice surrounding the disease. His decision unintentionally exposed his young children to stigma and discrimination. Read more about his story here.
